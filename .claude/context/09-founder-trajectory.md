# Founder Trajectory — Skills, Certifications, IIT Madras, Columbia, O-1B

## The Founder Profile Required for an $80M Exit

An acquirer buying a $15-20M ARR AI infrastructure product wants to see:
- Technical founder who built the methodology (not just a business person)
- Recognized voice in the AI reliability space (publications, GitHub, conference talks)
- Deep domain knowledge in at least one regulated vertical
- Track record of real client audits (ground truth database is evidence of this)

This is achievable from Thiruppuvanam, Tamil Nadu.
It requires building the right artifacts over 7-10 years.
Location is irrelevant for developer tools until late-stage exit.

## The Four Parallel Tracks (build simultaneously)

Track 1: Technical Skills
  Certifications + hands-on building
  Goal: genuine engineering authority, not just titles

Track 2: Domain Knowledge
  FinTech regulatory reading, FINRA rules, SEC compliance, HIPAA
  Goal: speak fluently with compliance officers, risk managers, CISOs

Track 3: Content + Writing
  1 technical article/month on AI reliability, MCP, cloud optimization
  Goal: become the person developers cite when they discuss AI agent reliability

Track 4: Network Credibility Through Output
  GitHub stars, HN submissions, LinkedIn posts, conference talks
  Goal: when you reach out to a CTO, they already know your name from your work

These four tracks run in parallel. They reinforce each other.
GitHub stars (Track 4) come from building (Track 1) + writing (Track 3).
Client trust (Track 2) comes from all four.

Program: Columbia University, Industrial Engineering and Operations Research (IEOR)
Application target: 2033 (when Fynor reaches $10M ARR and exit conversations begin)
GRE quant target: 166-170 (start preparation 18 months before application = 2031)

Application narrative:
  "I built an AI reliability company from scratch, self-funded it, and my product is
   applied Operations Research — here is 7 years of evidence:
   - IIT Madras BS in Data Science (2027-2031)
   - Ground truth database: 5,000+ labeled decisions (empirical OR laboratory)
   - Domain ontology: 500+ rules (constraint definitions)
   - Phase C platform: $10M ARR (applied feasibility solver)
   - IIT Madras research co-authorship on AI reliability as constraint satisfaction"

Why IEOR specifically:
  Operations Research is the mathematical framework for:
  - Optimization under constraints (Phase C domain rules = constraints)
  - Feasibility problems (is this agent's behavior within tolerance?)
  - Stochastic systems (ground truth database = empirical distribution)
  Fynor is an applied OR system. Columbia IEOR is the natural academic home.
